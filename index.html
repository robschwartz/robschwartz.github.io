First Site Review

May 14th 2014

Below are a list of my favorite sites. Some you might agree with, some you might find yourself lost in.

Reddit


It's a great source for pretty much anything you need. Just be careful when visiting, people tend to get sucked into the site and end up staying on there for much longer than they intended. The thumbnails are the most attractive to me. It gives you just enough of a preview to tickle your curiosity. The taglines are the most important but people gravitate to pictures faster than words.
  • Light
  • Roomy
  • Structured
  • Basic
  • Easy
It solves the horrible disease of having too much time. It has everything! Just do a quick search, I guarantee it has something you would be interested in. I definitely see this as a time-waster, but in a good way. That's the great thing about this site! You don't come here for a specific reason, you come to see what has changed since the last time you were there! You can choose different categories to specify what you want to see but I mainly scroll through the pages until something catches my eye. Extremely easy. No bells and whistles, only content. Like I had a good time but probably should have been doing something else It does not sell anything.

Dan's Deals


Ever since we moved from NY to Chicago, my wife (who is from Montreal) and I have been trying to find cheap and easy ways to fly her home. This site teaches you tricks and tips on how to manufacture spending and purchase cheap miles! Sometimes they just have really awesome deals. I flew from ORD to Montreal for $75 round trip!! The main content really attracts my eye the most. It is the most important area of the web page. Even though it's not very decorated, it gives me what I need.
  • Simple
  • Pleasent
  • Compact
  • Empty
  • Boring
The website is a simple blog about random internet deals, if you have a problem finding deals, this solves it. The content is mainly posts but he has a search bar as well as ads static to the top of the page Informative, money-saving, tricks of the trade, sneaky, useful Not always easy to find what I am looking for. They send out Tweets of all their deals and sometimes when I check the site for that deal, I can't always find it. I wish they would compact the site to content preview form to just show the title of the deal. It pretty easy to browse. All there is to do is scroll up and down. Bored, informed, confused, anxious, excited Doesn't sell anything

LinkedIn


It's a great way to stay in touch with friends and colleagues in a professional manor. The main news area called "Pulse" catches my eye the most. I don't believe this is the main point of the site but it is the biggest image on the page and it is directly in the middle. I see this site as Simple, spaced, readable, light, easy It solves the issue of people wanted to stay in touch on a professional level. Where Facebook and Twitter are more social, this stays professional. The content is made up of news articles related to the working professional, and working professionals themselves.
  • Practical
  • Informative
  • Social
  • Professional
  • Sturctued
It's pretty simple. If I am looking for a person or company I just do a search in the search bar at the top. Being more descriptive in the search is a great way to cut down the amount of people that show up. It can get frustrating if the person or company you are looking for doesn't have a profile. It's easy but a bit overwhelming. There's a lot to do on the site and if you don't have a set goal, you might get lost. Productive It sells deeper use of the website. I have not, I don't need it past the free use it allows

Why Wireframes??

May 16th 2014

Reddit

They are solving the problem of having a ton of content. They needed to figure out how much content to have in order to have it not be cluttered. Since the site is always changing, they need it to be consistent so people are familiar with the page when they return.

Dan's Deals

This site is similar to Reddit where they need to have constant updates since it is a relatively live blog. This site is more focused on the ads and content than the UI of the page. They just want to inform the user with their message and have them move on. Not too aestheticly pleasing but it gets their message across.

LinkedIn

This sites problem is understanding how to keep the user engaged. They have a ton of content but also need it remain professional since their main audience consists of working professionals. They also want to add relevant news, status updates, blog shares, people who you might be interested, and so on.

CSS Positioning

May 23rd 2014

What are the differences between relative, absolute, and fixed positioning?

One of the most frustrating concepts for my personal growth as a developer has been grasping the idea of positioning on CSS. Do I want the image to be Relative? Absolute? Fixed? Float? Absolute in relative? What does it all mean! Then once I figure out WHERE I want it to go, I need to figure out HOW I actually get it there! It's a journey of a process but little by little I am learning more about positioning and what each one means.
Here is a quick guide to understanding CSS positioning.

*First thing to note is all CSS starts in a static position. There are preset rules that are engrained in CSS that puts your HTML as it lays. No fancy positioning, just like typing on a typewriter.

Relative: Using relative positioning will allow you to move the block up, down, left or right relative to the webpage or the div you are working within. Remember that when you add in your numbers that it translates to, "I want the block this many pixels away from that side". So having a block Left: 20px, Top: 30px, it will show something like this:


Absolute: This type of positioning brings your item out of any div or block and puts it exactly where you want it to be on the webpage. If it was using space inside another block, it will give up its space to the items below it. Absolute position can be useful but you be careful since it can change the whole dynamic and design of the page. You also have to make sure it is not positioned on top of other items since it will overlap them. There is a way to fix this but we will discuss that another time.


Fixed : Fixed positioning is like that little thing in your eye that floats there no matter where you look. It is setting an element to a position and it will stay at that position no matter where you scroll on the screen. You have probably seen fixed positioning in action before but haven't noticed it. A good example of this can be seen here:
http://www.discovercg.com/index.php
(The top bar stays when you scroll up and down on the page)

I hope this bit of information broke it down for you and helped you understand the different types of positioning available. The best way to learn is by playing around with CSS and plugging in all the different styles to see what they do live. This is scratching the base of the mountain in terms of positioning but hopefully it helps on the journey to the top.

The basic format of the page will be a header which will be fixed to the top of the page. The header will be a way to rever back to the home page. Under the header will be the latest blog post. The post will have a header and the date under it. Content will follow.
